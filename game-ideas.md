# Rogue-Hero: Game Concept Ideas

## Core Design Philosophy
- **Easy to pick up, hard to master**
- Runs last 20–40 minutes
- Every death feels like a learning moment, not a punishment
- Combat should feel different every run

---

## Combat Loop Concepts

### Concept A: "Tempo" — Rhythm-Based Resource System
Every action you take (attack, dodge, skill) generates or costs **Tempo** — a shared meter that acts like a heartbeat.

- High Tempo = faster attacks, more damage, but enemies also act faster and hit harder
- Low Tempo = slow, methodical, enemies are sluggish too
- The skill is **choosing when to spike and when to slow down**
- Some builds want to live at max Tempo (berserker); others want to keep it near zero (assassin)
- Boss fights have Tempo phases — force you out of your comfort zone

**Why it's interesting:** Every fight is a negotiation of pace. It's readable at a glance but deeply strategic.

---

### Concept B: "Echo" — Your Past Self Fights With You
Each room you clear, a ghost of your last run-through replays in that room during future encounters.

- On your first visit, you fight alone
- On revisit or in loop NG+, your past self (the echo) is there — repeating your exact inputs
- You can **combo with your echo** or get in its way
- Echoes persist across rooms as haunting reminders of mistakes

**Why it's interesting:** Forces players to fight intentionally. You're literally cooperating with your history.

---

### Concept C: "Debt" — The Economy of Risk
Every action has a **Debt cost** — nothing is free.

- Attacking accrues Debt
- Taking damage reduces Debt (it "paid off")
- Dodging reduces Debt slightly
- If your Debt hits 0 you're invincible for a flash — **but if it spikes over max, you explode**
- Items and builds let you manipulate Debt caps, generation rate, and interest

**Why it's interesting:** Turns damage-avoidance on its head. Sometimes you *want* to get hit. High risk = high reward in a very tactile way.

---

### Concept D: "Pressure" — Zone Control Combat *(Recommended)*
The arena has a **Pressure grid** — invisible zones that build up when you or enemies stand in them.

- Standing still builds Pressure under you (bad — you'll get hit harder)
- Moving releases Pressure as shockwaves that damage nearby enemies
- Certain skills "vent" Pressure in bursts or channel it into a nuke
- Enemies have their own Pressure — lure them into high-Pressure zones for bonus damage

**Why it's interesting:** Movement IS the attack. Every step matters. It's immediately intuitive (don't stand still) but mastery is deep (routing, enemy herding, zone painting).

---

## Recommended Hybrid: "Pressure + Tempo"
Combine **Pressure** (zone control, movement-as-attack) with **Tempo** (pace negotiation):

- **Moving fast** builds Tempo but releases Pressure
- **Standing and charging** builds Pressure but drops Tempo
- The tension: do you zip around (high Tempo, low Pressure damage) or root and burst (low Tempo, huge Pressure nuke)?
- Boss encounters shift which playstyle is favored mid-fight

---

## Meta Progression Ideas
- **Echoes as unlocks** — defeated boss echoes become optional companions in future runs
- **Debt Ledger** — permanent record of how you've died; each death type unlocks a counter-item
- **Tempo Mastery** — hit certain Tempo thresholds consistently to unlock new skill tiers

---

## Visual & Feel Direction
- Top-down or isometric, clean readable visuals
- Each biome has a distinct Tempo "feel" (e.g., jungle = slow and oppressive, clockwork tower = frantic)
- Pressure zones visualized as heat-shimmer or color tinting on the floor
- Satisfying audio feedback for Tempo spikes and Pressure releases — the game should *sound* like it's breathing

---

## Simplicity Guardrails
- Max 2 active skills at a time
- Single resource bar visible at all times
- Every mechanic introduced with a 5-second tutorial room, never a text wall
- Color-coded danger: red = high Pressure/Tempo danger, blue = safe
